Week in Review

Shorter Hours Make
for a Sweet Week

With less time in the classroom each day, students found this week to be a breath of fresh air — and a perfect warm-up for spring break.

By Olivia • Staff Reporter • Managed by William

This week was unlike any other on the school calendar. With shorter hours in class each day, students and teachers found themselves working with a trimmed schedule — and the results were surprisingly wonderful. Less time in the building meant more focus, more energy, and a whole lot more excitement buzzing through the hallways.

Rather than the usual full day, the shortened classroom hours gave everyone a chance to breathe, recharge, and still get through everything that needed to be covered. Teachers packed their lessons with purpose, and students showed up ready to make every minute count. There was something energizing about knowing the day would wrap up a little earlier than normal.

"It felt like everyone was just a little more cheerful," noted one observer in the hallway. And it is hard to argue with that. Shorter hours have a way of raising spirits — especially when spring break is right around the corner and the countdown is very much on.

"Shorter hours, bigger smiles. That about sums up the week."

— Gwen, Staff Reporter

As the week draws to a close, students can look back with pride on what they accomplished in less time than usual. Homework was turned in, participation was strong, and the whole building had an undeniable spring-break-eve kind of energy. Well done to everyone — teachers and students alike.

Special Event

Little Buddies Brings
Big Smiles All Around

By Gwen & Olivia • Managed by William

This week's Little Buddies session was nothing short of wonderful. Students had the chance to spend time with their little buddies, and the joy on everyone's faces said everything that words cannot fully capture. Laughter filled the room, activities were shared, and bonds were strengthened in the way that only Little Buddies can manage.

Little Buddies is always one of the most anticipated events on the school calendar — and this week's session lived up to every expectation. Older students stepped up as role models, showing patience, creativity, and genuine care for their younger partners. The little ones, meanwhile, brought an infectious energy and enthusiasm that lit up the whole room.

Activities ranged from reading together to crafts and games, and every pair seemed to find their own special rhythm. Teachers overseeing the session noted how naturally the older students took to their mentoring roles, guiding their buddies with kindness and encouragement at every turn.

Why Little Buddies Matters

○  Builds leadership and responsibility in older students

○  Gives younger students a friendly, familiar face in school

○  Encourages kindness, patience, and communication

○  Creates memories that last well beyond the school year

"It was so fun," said one student after the session. "My little buddy was really funny and we had the best time." That sentiment was echoed up and down the room — smiles everywhere, high-fives at the door, and the quiet satisfaction of a day well spent together in good company.

"Little Buddies is one of those things that reminds you why school is great."

— Ryne, Staff Reporter
